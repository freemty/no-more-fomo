## Podcasts (Last 7 Days)
- **[Dwarkesh]** Terence Tao — Kepler, Newton, and the True Nature of Mathematical Discovery (Mar 20) | [link](https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/terence-tao) | [youtube](https://youtu.be/Q8Fkpi18QXU)
  > Tao compares Kepler to a "high-temperature LLM" — spending twenty years trying random hypotheses from Platonic solids to planetary harmonics, ultimately discovering elliptical orbits through Tycho Brahe's precision data. His core argument: AI has driven hypothesis generation cost to near-zero, but the bottleneck has shifted to verification — peer review systems are being overwhelmed by AI-generated submissions. He predicts human-AI hybrids will dominate mathematics for far longer than pure AI, because judging whether an idea has future value requires decades of cultural context that can't be captured in an RL loop.

- **[Latent Space]** Dreamer: the Personal Agent OS — David Singleton, former Stripe CTO (Mar 20) | [link](https://www.latent.space/p/dreamer) | [youtube](https://youtu.be/TvmxWWfiYWI)
  > Singleton unveils Dreamer, a consumer-first agent platform where the core "Sidekick" is an agent that builds agents. The platform ships a full stack — SDK, database, serverless functions, prompt management — and lets third-party tool builders earn revenue. The standout feature: agents generate daily briefing podcasts that push directly to Apple Podcasts. Drawing from his Android days at Google, Singleton is betting on a four-sided network effect between users, builders, tool creators, and the platform itself.

- **[Latent Space]** Felix Rieseberg — Why Anthropic Thinks AI Should Have Its Own Computer (Mar 17) | [link](https://www.latent.space/p/felix-anthropic)
  > Felix (Electron core maintainer, ex-Slack desktop lead) breaks down Claude Cowork: a VM-based knowledge work agent built in 10 days by orchestrating multiple Claude Code instances. Three key insights: (1) execution is now cheap enough to build multiple candidates and pick the winner instead of debating in meetings; (2) Skills (simple markdown instructions) are proving more flexible than rigid MCP tool schemas; (3) VM sandboxing creates a middle ground between "approve every command" and dangerous autonomy. Felix argues Silicon Valley is undervaluing local compute.
